How Common Is Each Nebraska High School Football Mascot?

football
sports
Author

John Russell

Published

May 4, 2025

High school mascots are more than just symbols. They become synonymous with the school and community that they represent. While many schools adopt common mascots like Eagles, Tigers or Bulldogs, others choose to stand out with unique mascots. Omaha Benson has the Bunnies, Fairbury has the Jeffs, and Columbus has the Discoverers. These mascots all tie into the community in a way that a more-common mascot wouldn’t and gives the community a sense of pride in their uniqueness.

library(tidyverse)
library(ggbeeswarm)
library(ggrepel)

data <- read_csv("data.csv")

mascot_enrollment_count <- data |> 
  
  group_by(MASCOT) |> 
  
summarise(
  Count=n(),
  Total=sum(ENROLLMENT),
  Average=mean(ENROLLMENT)
  )

mascot_enrollment_count_top20 <- mascot_enrollment_count |>
  arrange(desc(Total)) |>
top_n(20, wt=Total)

Bulldogs <- mascot_enrollment_count_top20 |>

Out of the 302 schools that competed in the 2024-25 NSAA High School football season, there were 98 different mascots across these teams. The most popular mascot choices among these schools were the generic, commonly-used ones that one would see in a movie or TV show. Eagles, Knights, Tigers and so on.

Schools using animals as mascots is as common of a choice as anything in sports, and Nebraska spotlights that idea. 7 of the 10 most-popular mascot choices are types of animals. 15 football teams in Nebraska call themselves the Eagles, with the largest being Omaha Central (1879 students) and the smallest being Bruning-Davenport (32 students).

With 98 different mascots, it becomes necessary to categorize and group them to better understand any trends. The mascots fall into 1 of 10 categories: Bird, Cat, Dog, Human, Insect, Other Animal, Object, Mythical, Weather/Nature, or Miscellaneous.

    CATEGORY == "Human"

Humans lead the way in this categorization of mascots. They have an abundance of schools with smaller enrollments as well as a healthy number of schools rising up the enrollment count.

Although Nebraska’s most-frequent mascots may be the common, “boring” ones, the state has plenty of options for unique/uncommon mascots as well. Out of the 98 total mascots, 46 schools have mascots that no other school in Nebraska has. These range across all 10 categories and all levels of enrollment. Some have local ties that only that only makes sense for that specific school (Centura Centurions, Pender Pendragons, Lincoln High Links). Some have seemingly no connection to the area unless you take a history lesson. Sutherland calls themselves the Sailors despite being nowhere near the ocean. Gothenburg calls themselves the Swedes despite being nowhere near Sweden. Both of those mascots become clearer with a short history lesson that might not be as well-known if it wasn’t for the conversation sparked by the mascots.

The mascots of Nebraska generally follow suit with the rest of the country. The generic, common names still reign supreme. Dogs, Cats, Humans and Birds make up the majority of mascots locally. But for every 10 Bulldogs, there’s a Omaha Benson Mighty Bunny, an Ord Chanticleer, a Minden Whippet, or a Fairbury Jeff reminding everyone that mascots can be unique too.
